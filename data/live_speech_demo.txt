Good afternoon, everyone, and thank you for joining me today.
I want to speak directly to the progress our state is making in these challenging times.
Our economic foundation is solid, and we are steering this state toward long-term prosperity.
Let's talk about the cost of living first.
Inflation has been a persistent challenge for all of our working families.
But I am proud to report that inflation has now dropped to 2.1% this quarter.
This shows that our supply chain policies and utility subsidies are delivering real relief.
At the same time, we have kept unemployment low.
As of this week, our state's unemployment rate stands at 5.9%.
We have created thousands of high-wage jobs in construction and technology.
Our public transit system is another core pillar of our economic plan.
I stand before you today to promise that we are completely freezing all transit ticket prices.
We will fully fund public transit expansions without any fare adjustments.
Transit is a public utility, and working families should not bear the cost of maintenance.
Housing is also a fundamental right.
My administration has been working tirelessly to build affordable homes.
To date, we have successfully constructed 18,500 affordable housing units.
We are on track to exceed our goals by the end of the fiscal year.
Finally, let's address our fiscal policy and taxes.
We need to fund our public education system properly.
Therefore, we are introducing a 3% income surcharge on households earning over $150,000.
This contribution will directly support our school districts and teachers.
But let me be absolutely clear: I pledge that we will not increase middle-class income taxes during my administration.
We will keep our promise to protect working class families from tax increases.
Thank you, and I look forward to your questions.
